So, Should We Keep Neutral?

Keep Neutral?

Yes, if the goal is business interpretation. Neutral prevents the system from forcing uncertain or mixed reviews into positive/negative.

Remove Neutral?

Yes, if the goal is a high-confidence polarity classifier. Removing ambiguous cases increased Macro F1 to 0.918.

Best Practical Choice

Use the binary model for alerting, and the cleaned 3-class model when neutral interpretation is needed.

The strongest improvement came from cleaning labels, not from adding model complexity.

Are These Results Reasonable?

External Benchmarks
  • SemEval-2013 Twitter message-level best F1 was about 69%.
  • SentiBench reports 3-class sentiment Macro F1 around 0.6 for best tools.
  • Neutral is repeatedly reported as harder than positive or negative.
Our Position
  • Original 3-class F1 0.720 is not unusually low for noisy neutral sentiment.
  • Cleaned 3-class F1 0.877 is strong for a self-collected weak-label dataset.
  • Binary F1 0.918 is suitable for production-style polarity alerting.
References: Nakov et al. (2013); Ribeiro et al. (2016); Dey et al. (2017); Northcutt et al. (2021).
